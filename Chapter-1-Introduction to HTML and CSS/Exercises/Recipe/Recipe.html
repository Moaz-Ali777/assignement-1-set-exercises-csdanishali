Karahi

Chicken Karahi is a rich, aromatic dish from Pakistani and North Indian cuisine. Cooked in a wok with chicken, tomatoes, yogurt, and bold spices, it has a thick, flavorful gravy with a signature kick from green chilies and black pepper. Garnished with ginger and coriander, it’s best enjoyed with naan or roti. A must-try for spice lovers!

Ingredients

  • ½ kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 3 tomatoes (chopped or blended)
  • 1 medium onion (finely sliced) [optional]
  • 2 tbsp yogurt
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2-3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp black pepper powder
  • Salt (for taste)
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger (julienned)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped, for garnish)

Method

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a karahi (wok) over medium heat.
  2. Add chicken and sauté until it changes color and slightly browns.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften and break down (you can blend them for a smoother texture).
  5. Add yogurt, salt, and spices (red chili, turmeric, cumin, coriander) and cook until oil separates.
  6. Add green chilies and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Sprinkle black pepper and garam masala, mix well.
  8. >Garnish with fresh coriander and julienned ginger.
  9. Serve hot with naan or roti.
